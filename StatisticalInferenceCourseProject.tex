\subsection{Overview}\label{overview}

The purpose of this project is to investigate the exponential
distribution in R and compare it with the Central Limit Theorem. The
exponential distribution will be simulated in R with rexp(n, lambda),
where lambda is the rate parameter. The mean of the exponential
distribution is 1/lambda and the standard deviation is also 1/lambda.
Lambda = 0.2 for all simulations.

In this project, I will investigate the distribution of averages of 40
exponentials and do 1,000 simulations.

\subsection{Simulations}\label{simulations}

First we will set up the simulation parameters.

\begin{Shaded}
\begin{Highlighting}[]
\KeywordTok{set.seed}\NormalTok{(}\DecValTok{1111}\NormalTok{)}
\NormalTok{lambda =}\StringTok{ }\FloatTok{0.2}
\NormalTok{n =}\StringTok{ }\DecValTok{40}
\end{Highlighting}
\end{Shaded}

Next, we will run the simulations and calculate the means.

\begin{Shaded}
\begin{Highlighting}[]
\NormalTok{sims <-}\StringTok{ }\KeywordTok{replicate}\NormalTok{(}\DecValTok{1000}\NormalTok{, }\KeywordTok{rexp}\NormalTok{(n, lambda))}
\NormalTok{sims_means <-}\StringTok{ }\KeywordTok{apply}\NormalTok{(sims, }\DecValTok{2}\NormalTok{, mean)}
\end{Highlighting}
\end{Shaded}

\subsection{Sample Mean vs.~Theoretical
Mean}\label{sample-mean-vs.theoretical-mean}

The sample mean is the mean of the simulation means

\begin{Highlighting}[]

\NormalTok{sample_mean}
\end{Highlighting}
\end{Shaded}
